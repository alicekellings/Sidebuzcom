# How to Make Money Fast as a Student

Rent was due in 5 days. I had $87 in my account.

Needed $600. Fast.

Tried everything. Sold my textbooks. Picked up extra shifts. Donated plasma twice.

Made $620 in 4 days.

Here's exactly what I did and what actually works when you need money NOW.

## The Situation: When You Need Money This Week

Let me be clear about something first.

This isn't about building a sustainable income. This is about emergency money.

Rent due. Car broke down. Emergency flight home. Medical bill.

You need $200-500 in the next 7 days.

Here's what actually works.

## Method 1: Sell Your Stuff (Made $180 in 2 Days)

I know. Obvious. But hear me out.

Not your old clothes. Not your random junk. Your VALUABLE stuff.

**What I sold**:
- Calculus textbook: $85 (bought for $200)
- Nintendo Switch games: $60 (3 games at $20 each)
- Desk lamp: $15
- Mini fridge: $20

**Total**: $180 in 2 days

**Where to sell**:
- Facebook Marketplace (fastest)
- Campus Facebook groups (students have cash)
- OfferUp (local pickup)
- Craigslist (older buyers, more cash)

**Pro tips**:
- Price 20% below market value (sells instantly)
- "Cash only, pickup today"
- Meet in public places
- Post in the morning (more views)

**What sells fast**:
- Textbooks (always)
- Electronics (gaming stuff, headphones)
- Furniture (mini fridges, desk chairs)
- Bikes
- Parking passes

## Method 2: Plasma Donation ($100 in 3 Hours)

This saved me.

First-time donors get bonuses. I made $100 for my first donation.

**How it works**:
1. Find a plasma center (CSL Plasma, BioLife, Grifols)
2. Bring ID and proof of address
3. Pass health screening
4. Donate (takes 90 minutes)
5. Get paid immediately

**First-time bonuses**: $50-100 for first donation, $50-100 for second

**Regular pay**: $30-50 per donation

**Frequency**: Twice a week maximum

**Reality check**: It's not fun. Needles. Time commitment. But it's fast money.

I made $100 my first visit, $80 my second visit (3 days later). That's $180 in one week.

## Method 3: Gig Apps ($140 in 8 Hours)

Downloaded DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Instacart.

Worked Friday night and Saturday. Made $140.

**Friday night** (5pm-9pm): $65
**Saturday** (11am-3pm): $75

**Why it worked**:
- Friday dinner rush (high tips)
- Saturday lunch (lots of orders)
- Stayed in busy areas

**Requirements**:
- Car (or bike in some cities)
- Clean driving record
- 18+ years old
- Smartphone

**Pro tips**:
- Work during meal times (11am-2pm, 5pm-9pm)
- Stay near restaurants
- Accept orders over $7
- Multi-app (run DoorDash and Uber Eats simultaneously)

**Realistic earnings**: $15-25/hour depending on area and time

## Method 4: Facebook Marketplace Flipping ($120 in 3 Days)

This one's sneaky good for fast money.

Found underpriced items on Facebook Marketplace. Bought them. Resold them same day.

**Example 1**:
- Bought desk chair for $20
- Cleaned it
- Sold for $50 same day
- Profit: $30

**Example 2**:
- Bought textbook for $30
- Sold to classmate for $80
- Profit: $50

**Example 3**:
- Bought mini fridge for $25
- Sold for $65
- Profit: $40

**Total profit**: $120 in 3 days

**What to look for**:
- Items listed way below market value
- "Moving, must sell today"
- Misspelled listings (less competition)
- Items you can clean/fix easily

**Best items**: Furniture, textbooks, electronics, bikes

## Method 5: Campus Odd Jobs ($80 in 4 Hours)

Posted in campus Facebook group: "Need help moving, cleaning, or random tasks? $20/hour."

Got 3 responses:

**Job 1**: Help someone move (2 hours, $40)
**Job 2**: Clean apartment before inspection (1.5 hours, $30)
**Job 3**: Assemble IKEA furniture (30 minutes, $10)

**Total**: $80 for 4 hours of work

**Other quick jobs students need**:
- Moving help
- Cleaning
- Furniture assembly
- Tech help (printer setup, computer issues)
- Yard work
- Pet sitting

**How to find them**: Campus Facebook groups, Nextdoor, Craigslist gigs section

## Method 6: Return Stuff You Don't Need ($60)

Looked through my room. Found stuff I bought but never used.

**Returned**:
- Hoodie from last week: $35 refund
- Textbook I bought but didn't need: $25 refund

**Total**: $60 back on my card

**Pro tip**: Most stores have 30-90 day return policies. Check your receipts.

## Method 7: Ask for Advance Payment ($200)

I tutor calculus. Usually get paid weekly.

Texted my 3 tutoring clients: "Hey, any chance you could pay me for next week in advance? Having a cash flow issue."

All three said yes. Venmo'd me immediately.

**Total**: $200 (would've gotten it next week anyway, but needed it NOW)

**Who to ask**:
- Regular clients
- Freelance clients
- Anyone who owes you money

Most people understand. Just be honest.

## Method 8: Pawn Shop ($150 Loan)

Took my laptop to a pawn shop. They gave me $150 loan.

**How it works**:
- Bring valuable item
- They assess value
- Give you 25-50% of value as loan
- You have 30-90 days to buy it back (with interest)

**What I did**: Got $150 loan on my $600 laptop. Paid $165 to get it back 2 weeks later.

**Cost**: $15 for 2 weeks of emergency cash

**What they take**: Electronics, jewelry, musical instruments, tools

**Reality check**: High interest. Only use if you're SURE you can pay it back.

## Method 9: Emergency Shift Pickup ($100)

Texted my old campus job: "Any shifts available this week? Need extra hours."

They had someone call out. Worked 8 hours at $12.50/hour.

**Made**: $100 after taxes

**Where to look**:
- Old jobs (they know you)
- Campus dining halls (always need people)
- Campus events (setup/breakdown)
- Retail stores (call-outs happen constantly)

## The Mistakes I Made

### Mistake 1: Payday Loans

Considered a payday loan. $300 loan, pay back $390 in 2 weeks.

That's 30% interest for 2 weeks. Insane.

Did the math. Would've been stuck in a debt cycle.

**Never do payday loans.** The other methods are better.

### Mistake 2: Selling Too Cheap

Panic-sold my textbook for $85. Could've gotten $120 if I waited a day.

Lost $35 because I was desperate.

**Lesson**: Price fairly, not desperately. You'll still sell fast.

### Mistake 3: Not Asking for Help

Waited 3 days before asking my tutoring clients for advance payment.

Should've asked immediately. Would've had the money sooner.

**Lesson**: People want to help. Just ask.
