Every "side hustle for students" article recommends the same stuff. But when exactly am I supposed to do this between lectures and group projects?

Here's what actually works for busy students.

## 1. Homework Help Platforms

Answer academic questions, get paid. No scheduling - work between classes or at 11pm.

**Earnings**: $200-$800/month

## 2. Freelance Writing

Content writing - blogs, product descriptions. Pick gigs that match your schedule.

**Earnings**: $300-$1,000/month

## 3. Virtual Assistant (Micro Tasks)

Small tasks - data entry, research. 15-60 minutes each.

**Earnings**: $150-$400/month

## 4. Selling Class Notes

Upload your notes, earn when someone downloads. Zero extra time.

**Earnings**: $100-500/semester

## 5. Online Tutoring

Set availability, students book sessions. You control your schedule.

**Earnings**: $300-$800/month
